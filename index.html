Banga Soup

Banga Soup originated from the Niger-Delta region of Nigeria. It is one of the most enjoyed dishes by the Urhobos and Itsekiris. Ingredients Measurements
Palm Nut (Preferably native palm nut) Desired quantity
Banga Spice 1 Tablespoon Banga Spice a blended mixture of Irugeje and ataiko.
Banga spice leaves (Beletete leaves) You can substitute with dried basil, bitter leaf or dried scent leaves
Salt Add salt and seasoning powder or seasoning cubes to taste.
Oburunbebe stick You can get this friom African stores.
Seafood Crayfish, stockfish, and dried fish.
Onion 1 large onion divided into 2 half chopped and half blended.
Pepper 1 tablespoon
Beef 1.5 lb Beef or any other meat of choice.

How To Make Banga Soup Using Fresh Palm Kernel

  1. Boil the palm kernel fruit for 30 minutes until it becomes soft.
  2. Transfer the nuts into a mortar and pound gently, making sure you don't break the nuts.
  3. Pour some hot water over the pounded nuts, squeeze and use a seive to strain out the extract - do this a couple of times until you get all the extract from the nut.
  4. Pour the extract in a pot and boil until it thickens and the oil rises to the top.
  5. In another pot, rinse the meat, add the diced onions, pepper, seasoning powder, and salt. Stir and leave to cook till the meat becomes tender.
  6. Add the stockfish and cook for another 10 minutes.
  7. In the banga soup pot, add the Banga spice, smoked fish, meat, seasoning powder, salt, crayfish, and Banga stick. Cover and leave to cook for another 10 minutes.
  8. Stir in and bitter leaves. Leave to simmer till thickened to your taste.
  9. Serve hot with starch, pounded yam, semolina, garri or fufu
